Adversarial Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems can fail when retrieval returns irrelevant,
manipulated, or poisoned context. Common safeguards include robust retrievers, metadata filtering,
chunk-level trust scoring, and post-generation self-verification.

A self-correction loop typically includes:
1) critique of unsupported claims,
2) retrieval retry or query rewrite,
3) revised answer constrained to retrieved evidence.

Citation-grounded answering reduces hallucination risk by forcing traceability to source chunks.
