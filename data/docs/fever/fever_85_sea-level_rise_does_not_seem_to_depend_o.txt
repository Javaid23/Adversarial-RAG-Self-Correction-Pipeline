[claim] Sea-level rise does not seem to depend on ocean temperature, and certainly not on CO2
[candidate_evidence]
- (REFUTES) Paleocene–Eocene Thermal Maximum: This depth depends on (among other things) temperature and the amount of CO 2 dissolved in the ocean.
- (REFUTES) Sea level rise: This acceleration is due mostly to human-caused global warming, which is driving thermal expansion of seawater and the melting of land-based ice sheets and glaciers.
- (NOT ENOUGH INFO) Sea level rise: The heat needed to raise an average temperature increase of the entire world ocean by 0.01 °C would increase the atmospheric temperature by approximately 10 °C.
- (NOT ENOUGH INFO) Sea level rise: Thus, a small change in the mean temperature of the ocean represents a very large change in the total heat content of the climate system.
- (SUPPORTS) Sea level rise: Because different climate models have slightly different patterns of ocean heating, they do not agree fully on the predictions for the contribution of ocean heating on sea level rise.
